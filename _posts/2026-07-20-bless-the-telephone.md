This song has been running in my mind lately, I hope you can listen to it

So before I watched the movie, some songs were really hitting close, I resonated with some of its lyrics and melodies and I continued to think about how things happened the way they did. I will explain what each song made me feel but first, I want to address that I watched the movie as soon as I could and had the strength to do it. Because I broke down in tears reading your text about the movie, and I couldn't wait to see it and understand why it made you feel the way it made you feel, and even before watching it I felt like it robbed some words out of my mouth from the things you wrote. 

I can't stop thinking of you MIlitta, about the way you'd think about something too either, about the way your hands felt in mine, how I dreamt of waking up next to you this time we shared and for the rest of my life. I kept thinking as well the whole time for us and it really struck me how many things really made me think of us while watching. It specially burst in me the way in the scene after the photos in the stairs, the thought of living together stayed until the last moment of this character. I've felt like dying inside too but still this desire has stayed within me and will stay within me all my life, life gives us signals and messages where we least expect it, and its by itself just such a beautiful coincidence that this stumbled upon you and upon me as a result of it. I can't say much more than what the film told you and made you feel, I felt it too, and I don't want to lose you either, I never wanted to lose you, you were and you will be the love of my life until the end MIlitta.

As you can see the text started with the song because this was before I watched the movie, I had some songs and some small messages to write about each of them for this text, so I'll just continue with what I started although chronologically that was the last one that I was replaying more often.

This song, *La Puerta - Los Tres Ases* it's from this kind of genre of music from the 30's-40's in Mexico, this genre since I was a teenager was something that I was really drawn for although many of the people my age considered it like 'old people music'. And it may be true but it captures some sense of love and romanticism that has long been lost by modernity, and a sense of longing and melancholia that only this kind of sound captures and is not findable elsewhere. I think there is no need to explain the lyrics as they are pretty self-explanatory but I felt this sense of abandonment until you wrote me again these two last texts.

The other song that comes before this one, was *Fog(Again) by Radiohead* this song really stuck with me some days, I don't know if there is any point in mentioning it because I feel myself wordless about it for now.

the third song I wanted to talk about just, I don't know where to begin. But this time where I felt so alone, that we weren't speaking while this whole situation was unfolding, and the trip was about to come up. One of these days of longing for you a song by ironically the same artist that appears at the end of Sentimental Value, the first movie of this director we just saw as well, appeared in my playlist while showering and I remember I couldn't hold my composure either, every lyric of this song teared me down and a sense of missing you invaded me entirely and completely. And although chronologically was the first one, I mention it last because no other song has made me felt this way ever since everything started going down. 

The song is *Bless The Telephone - Labi Siffre*
