The days haven't gotten any easier, and yet also neither the way to write you this first text on our website. I miss you in any display of sweetness I see in real life or in the internet, I get reminded of you in the most evident places but it also seems that my brain connects you to even the most remote things. Days and thoughts are a endless pathway towards you, towards what we were and I always stay missing you in every single second of these times. That is not to say that I'm the most resilient because I do fall into habits and thoughts that I don't want to fall into, it's the limbic system within me in fear of how life has gotten the love of my life, the person I've cared for the most, far from me; a distance not characterized for it's physical reality, but a distance created by how life has steered us to decisions and habits that have hurt us. 



This by no means is a proof of an endless pessimism, because, I do believe that we can still recover what we had, and the testament is in the numerous times I've came back to talk about it, and as lastly said, I believe in a last chance to make this work, I want this to work, and we will make it with effort and patience.



This will be now, an untraditional way to communicate because we remove the immediateness of how messaging apps work like now, we remove the quick feedback response out of the equation and we're falling into a way of writing that more closely resembles the way people used to communicate in correspondence a long time ago. But my sentiment that the most meaningful things take time and effort are showcased here in this exact same example, this way of communicating will teach us to take time of our day writing something in full conscience, every word has to count because as in paper, you need to fully express an idea in the space of a letter. I think it will allows us to more transparently open our heart and mind in each word we want to communicate with each other, the act of writing this sort of letters is not only a channel for communication but also a way to truly delve into our thoughts and acts as a way of introspection. This, by itself, is an act that will teach us a lot down the road.



It's an entirely big topic I could spent hours writing about, about the danger of digitalism, which in my opinion has hurt us plenty already, but I will present you here the things I want to achieve while trying to do meaningful stuff that I think could make me grow. It's of course necessary to understand that many things that do us good aren't precisely the things that -at the beginning- are the most engaging or entertaining at first glance, but surely again, with patience, they offer us something that the immediateness of social media couldn't give us for our future. 



\- read a random article per week outside from my interests

\- sit in silence for 10 minutes per day pondering about us, or about an idea

\- change the environment where I feel I could fall back into a bad habit

\- trying to speak slower on purpose to make all my conversation not a reflex but the result of conscious thought

\- try out, from Seneca letters this thing called \*premeditatio malorum\* this is an ancient greek stoic method of meditation, which opposite as it suggests, is not about being negative but preparing against everything bad that could happen, if you happen to read about this I'd like to know your thoughts about it

\- practice 'box breathing' for tension

\- start a physical journal, you can use the notebook I gifted to you, you can draw what you see outside, and write there and take pictures and add it to the texts you could write to me here, I will do the same. there is a culture of doing this in the architecture world, they call people that do this 'flâneur'

\- I also read that visualization helps a lot with achieving things we want, that is, stopping a habit or becoming better at something, there's apparently some backed up data to suggest that imagining oneself doing the thing we want to achieve, prepares the nervous system to execute as planned, tell me your thoughts about this as well

\- Start a book but write in it, a book without any writing done in it I think, doesn't help much, writing a index at the beginning of the ideas per chapter or in specific pages helps link easier the biggest ideas that the book tries to give without getting lost in the details

\- I tried to put more peculiar things to try out but the rest of the list basically is, me trying to learn the languages I've been neglecting myself to learn because I've done social media instead

\- watch meaningful movies

\- skip online multiplayer games and put time into single player story-driven games for entertainment

\- regain this interest in discovering music, so once per week I'll try to listen to a new album I haven't hear before

\- Try some habit that it's also unconventional, I will try to learn again origami, and I will try to learn again calligraphy

\- I'm also trying to replace cheap youtube videos for some more interesting videos, philosophy classes and the architecture/history videos we watched back when we were at Florence

\- taking time alone, walking alone and exercising alone, I feel that there are times where we need to coexist only with ourselves and our thoughts and these are the times were we learn the most about the way we act consciously or subconsciously



I am conscious that all at the same time is not possible, but the constant effort for something more meaningful is a good start, so I hope I could have given you some ideas with this, for you this may also mean learn new recipes as I know you enjoy cooking/baking, or learning Spanish if you genuinely are interested in learning so. Obviously taking time to write for yourself and understand yourself is vital, I just expressed what I'll do, but I'm sure you will find ways so that you can feel you're growing in the aspects of your life you want to grow in. 



I'll try to write you at least, once a week, so you can see how life is changing for me, little by little, and I would like the same from you, I'm sure that with intention, we can be what we once were. 



I love you with my soul Militta, and I will always do.

Best, 

Ivan
